INDIAN RELAY is an inspiring portrait of three American Indian families as they prepare for and compete in one of the most exciting and dangerous forms of horse-racing practiced anywhere in the world today: Indian Relay. This story builds in intensity as preparations in the cold off-season give way to the high-risk summer races where ambulances are on standby. The film culminates at the National Championships while we hold our breath for one of our teams to walk away the winner. Inches of Snow and Tide: Climate Change in the Interconnected Landscapes of Olympic National Park ( 2010 )
Co-editor

For Olympic National Park by Open Boat Films

It's not the change of climate change that is the issue, it's the rate of the change. The Olympic Peninsula is a tough, diverse, and colorful ecosystem to call home. Meet passionate, quirky, and personable scientists at Olympic National Park as they divulge the secret life of coastal and mountain ecosystems with viewers. Explore tide pools in the intertidal zone with ecologist Steve Fradkin as he introduces us to Purple Urchins, 80-year old Giant Green Anemones, and the icon of the Pacific Northwest, the Sea Star. Learn how snowmelt in the mountains induces sea level rise and how it will change the composition, diversity, and food sources of the coastal inhabitants. Come away fascinated with macro-algae, mussels, clams, and snow-pack.

Fundraising/Branding Video ( 2014 )
Editor

For Western Sustainability Exchange

Western Sustainability Exchange was founded in 1994 on the principles of preserving the abundance of the Northern Rockies including its open spaces, wildlife habitats, water resources, rural communities, and agricultural land and heritage. Today, WSE boasts numerous successes in bringing together concerned parties who are committed to the future of Montana. The organization has aided farmers and ranchers in designing and implementing innovative and sustainable production strategies, developed value-added markets for these producers, and educated thousands of consumers about the long-term benefits of sustainable purchasing.

An American's Guide to Breastfeeding ( Post-production )
Writer/Director/Editor

A satirical critique of Americans' simultaneous prudishness and obsession with sex takes aim at how that obsession affects breastfeeding women and generates society's myths about breastfeeding. By blending satire and documentary into a new style of creative non-fiction, An American's Guide to Breastfeeding links breastfeeding to terrorism, economic woes, and sexual dysfunction.
