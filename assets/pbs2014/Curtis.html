** Why We Should Never Pay Down the National Debt

In 2012, Neil H. Buchanan, George Washington University Law School, wrote

“… because the federal government can undertake high-return investments (such as spending on education and infrastructure), the Golden Rule of government budgeting suggests that the federal government should run annual deficits to finance such long-term investments” 

For example, Buchanan reports that for every $1 spent to prevent students from dropping out of school, $1.45-$3.55 was returned to the government, potentially saving the government $90bil/year

and concludes:

“we should not only ignore calls to balance the budget or to pay down the national debt, but we should engage in a responsible plan to increase the national debt each year. Only by issuing debt to lubricate the financial system, and to support the economy’s healthy growth, can we guarantee a prosperous future for current and future citizens of the United States.” (Pg. 697)

Neil H. Buchanan Argues that the return on our investments is greater than the initial cost, thus ensuring a prosperous future for our children.

Climate/Environment

  1. How do you reconcile the economic impact of coal production, especially in tribal communities, with is contributing role in climate change?
    1. (Coal brings money, yes, but the burning of coal adds carbon dioxide to the atmosphere and is a major contributor to climate change.)

 

As adopted by a Constitutional Convention and ratified by Montanans in 1972, the Montana Constitution provides for certain inalienable rights, including “the right to a clean and healthful environment in Montana.” Justice Terry N. Trieweiler, writing for the Montana Supreme Court, concluded that the Montana Constitution protects not only real damages to the environment, but also anticipated pollution. This makes the constitutional protections both anticipatory and preventive. In his opinion he stated, “Our constitution does not require that dead fish float on the surface of our state’s rivers and streams before its farsighted environmental protections can be invoked.”

  1. So, is continuing or increasing natural resource extraction, such as coal or natural gas through fracking, contrary to our right set forth by our state constitution?
